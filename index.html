Research Interests

I am a PhD researcher in computational biology and bioinformatics, specializing in AI-driven genomics. My research focuses on how arboviruses evolve and adapt to different hosts, leveraging machine learning, deep learning, and high-performance computing to analyze codon usage patterns, predict viral evolution, and optimize protein expression.

I integrate genomics, transcriptomics, and proteomics with LLMs/NLP for genomics, developing predictive models for host-virus interactions with applications in precision medicine, antiviral strategies, and synthetic biology.

About Me (CV)

I received my M.S. in Biomedical Science and Engineering from Gwangju Institute of Science and Technology, Korea, where my thesis focused on unsupervised machine learning approaches for analyzing 3D behavioral patterns in diabetic neuropathy mouse models. I also hold a B.S. in Biomedical Engineering from Jimma University, Ethiopia, graduating in the top 2% of my class.
