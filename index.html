Welcome to My Research Website

I am a PhD Biomedical Engineering student at the University of Nebraska–Lincoln , advised by Prof. Nicole Sexton.

Research Interests

I am specializing in AI-driven genomics. My research focuses on how arboviruses evolve and adapt to different hosts, leveraging machine learning, deep learning, and high-performance computing to analyze codon usage patterns, predict viral evolution, and optimize protein expression. My research integrates genomics, transcriptomics, and proteomics with LLMs/NLP for genomics, developing predictive models for host-virus interactions with applications in precision medicine, antiviral strategies, and synthetic biology.

About Me (CV)

Prior to my PhD, I obtained my M.S. in Biomedical Science and Engineering from Gwangju Institute of Science and Technology , Korea. My thesis focused on revealing intricate 3D behavioral patterns in diabetic neuropathy mice using unsupervised machine learning algorithms, such as autoregressive models and hidden Markov models. I also hold a B.S. in Biomedical Engineering from Jimma University , Ethiopia, graduating in the top 2% of the school.
