News

Jul 2026 Released EnhancerDiff, a discrete-diffusion generator of cell-type-specific regulatory DNA that reuses the ProtDiff-ESM machinery re-pointed from protein to DNA. Trained on the Gosai 2024 MPRA, it designs 200 bp enhancers for K562, HepG2, and SK-N-SH, then validates them with the OracleGap suite against the real Malinois K562 (target) and Enformer K562 DNase (independent) oracles via Proto. The K562 and HepG2 galleries hold up under the independent oracle (near-zero independence gap), SK-N-SH shows a larger gap, and all designs are highly novel (mean novelty 0.94 to 0.96) rather than memorized copies of natural enhancers.
Jun 2026 Released SpliceConsensus, a reproducible benchmark of splicing variant-effect predictors against a real experimental splicing assay, revealing a shared exon-interior blind spot. On 27,733 exonic variants from the MFASS multiplexed assay (Cheung 2019), 22 percent of splice-disrupting variants (228 of 1,050) are missed by every tool, and roughly 79 percent of those sit in the exon interior, consistent with ESE/ESS disruptions that splice-site models cannot see. Among single predictors Pangolin ranks highest (AUROC 0.888), ahead of SpliceAI and SpliceTransformer, and a calibrated consensus does not improve on Pangolin alone. Predictors are run through Proto, and every number is reproducible from the released data.
Jun 2026 Released OracleGap, an open-source oracle-independence and novelty evaluation suite for generative DNA design with Proto (Hie lab, Arc Institute). It tests whether Proto's Malinois-optimized K562 enhancers hold up under an independent oracle (Enformer K562 DNase) and are novel rather than memorized; on this benchmark the designs transfer to the independent oracle, reported with honest caveats that agreement between two in-silico models is not wet-lab validation.
Jun 2026 Released AbStab, an open-source leakage-aware benchmark for sequence-based antibody thermostability (Tm) prediction. On the Jain 2017 clinical-antibody set, random train/test splits substantially overstate generalization to novel antibodies, and a general protein language model (ESM-2) relies far more on clonal leakage than an antibody-specific model (AntiBERTy).
Jun 2026 Released PerturbVAE, an open-source leakage-aware benchmark for single-cell perturbation-response prediction: a conditional VAE evaluated under random, function-grouped, and combinatorial held-out splits on the Norman 2019 and Replogle 2022 Perturb-seq datasets.

About

My research sits at the intersection of machine learning and genomics. I develop foundation models and rigorous evaluation frameworks for biological sequence analysis, with applications in viral emergence prediction, host-range classification, and model interpretability. My work increasingly spans generative modeling, where I apply discrete diffusion and classifier-free guidance techniques to design protein variants with targeted functional properties. I work primarily with PyTorch, Hugging Face Transformers, and the SciPy/PyData ecosystem on HPC GPU clusters.

Prior to my PhD, I earned an M.Sc. in Biomedical Science and Engineering from Gwangju Institute of Science and Technology (GIST), South Korea, where I applied unsupervised machine learning (autoregressive models, Hidden Markov models) to 3D behavioral phenotyping in diabetic neuropathy mouse models. I also hold a B.Sc. in Biomedical Engineering from Jimma University, Ethiopia, graduating in the top 2% of my Biomedical Engineering class.

When I'm not at the computer, you'll find me exploring coffee shops ☕ or at the gym 🏋.
